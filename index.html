Cloning Survey Research Project

A comprehensive study on public perception of cloning technologies and bioethics

How This Survey Was Conducted

This survey was conducted among a diverse sample of participants aged 14–18 across multiple academic environments. Respondents were shown four animals—a pig, sheep, gaur, and dog. Two of these animals (the sheep and gaur) were cloned specimens, while the pig and dog were normal, naturally born animals. Participants were instructed to judge whether the animals appeared cloned or normal, respond to ethical questions about cloning, and provide deeper moral perspectives in long‑form comments. All responses were collected anonymously, and data were processed without modification or weighting.
